hall; (hôl): a corridor or passage in a building.
pillar; (ˈpilər): a tall vertical structure used as a support for a building.
alley; (ˈalē): a narrow passageway behind or between buildings.
decay; (dəˈkā): to slowly deteriorate.
railway; (ˈrālˌwā): a track made of steel rails along which trains run.
fountain; (ˈfount(ə)n): an ornamental structure in a pool or lake from which one or more jets of water are pumped into the air.
balcony; (ˈbalkənē): a platform enclosed by a wall or balustrade on the outside of a building, with access from an upper-floor window or door.
fort; (fôrt): a fortified building or strategic position.
